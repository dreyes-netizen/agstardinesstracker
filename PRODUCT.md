# Product

## Register

product

## Users

HR managers, supervisors, and team leads at Alliance Global Solutions (AGS). Internal use only — accessed on desktop at the office. Users are task-oriented: they need to quickly see who is late, how often, and act on it. They are not designers and will not tolerate a confusing interface.

## Product Purpose

The AGS Tardiness Tracker ingests Sprout HR attendance exports, identifies employees who have crossed the tardiness threshold (6+ lates or 60+ accumulated minutes per month), and manages the full NTE (Notice to Explain) lifecycle from issuance to acknowledgment. Success means: a supervisor uploads a report, immediately sees who needs action, and issues an NTE in under a minute.

## Brand Personality

Professional, clear, authoritative. The tool should feel like a well-run office: nothing flashy, nothing wasted. It speaks with confidence — if the system says an NTE is required, the user trusts it.

## Anti-references

- **Workday / SAP SuccessFactors**: Bulky, enterprise-beige, cluttered. Every action buried three menus deep.
- **Generic HR SaaS dashboards**: Rounded cards, pastel palette, hero metrics with gradient accents, feels like a startup demo.
- **Heavy data viz tools**: This is an action tool, not a reporting tool. No pie charts, no animation for its own sake.

## Design Principles

1. **The data is the UI** — every pixel of chrome should earn its place by making the data clearer.
2. **Action surfaces, not report surfaces** — the dashboard tells you who needs attention; everything else follows.
3. **Earned familiarity** — use conventions HR staff already know (tables, badges, drawers). Delight is in precision, not novelty.
4. **Authority without aggression** — red means action required, not alarm. The system guides, not panics.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard desktop use. WCAG AA contrast targets as a practical floor (users may be in varying lighting). No known motor or visual impairment accommodations beyond standard keyboard navigation.
